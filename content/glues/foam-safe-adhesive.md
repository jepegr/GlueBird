# Foam-Safe Adhesive: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

Foam has a reputation problem: most of the adhesives that work great on everything else will actively eat through it. Foam-safe adhesive exists specifically to avoid that failure.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- The classic demonstration of the problem is nail polish remover on a styrofoam cup — the acetone dissolves the foam almost instantly, and many solvent-based glues share the same chemistry that causes it.
- Contact cement, standard super glue, and most spray adhesives all carry some risk to polystyrene and EVA foam, which is why foam-specific formulas exist as their own product category rather than a footnote on general adhesives.
- Foam-safe adhesives typically rely on water-based or specially formulated solvent systems engineered not to attack the foam's cell structure.

## Best Uses

- Craft foam, EVA foam, and polystyrene projects
- Any bond involving foam, regardless of the other material
- Cosplay, prop-making, and foam-core board projects

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- When in doubt, test on a foam offcut first — a dissolving surface shows up within seconds.
- Hot glue works in a pinch on sturdier foams but can melt a shallow crater into soft ones — use a low-temp gun.
- Craft glue is a safe fallback if you can clamp the joint while it dries.
- Avoid anything labeled contact cement or standard super glue on foam, no matter how tempting the strength claim.
