# Hot Glue: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

Hot glue is the instant-gratification adhesive — no clamping, no curing time, just a squeeze of the trigger and a bond that sets in under a minute.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- Thermoplastic hot-melt adhesives were developed in the mid-20th century originally for industrial packaging lines, long before the glue gun became a craft-table staple.
- Because it's thermoplastic rather than chemically curing, a hot glue bond can be re-melted and repositioned — which is exactly why it's not considered a permanent, structural adhesive.
- Low-temperature glue guns exist specifically because standard hot glue runs hot enough to warp foam and thin plastics on contact.

## Best Uses

- Fast craft assembly and temporary positioning
- Foam and fabric where a low-temp gun is used
- Anywhere a re-workable bond is more useful than a permanent one

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- Use a low-temp gun on foam, ribbon, and delicate fabric — a standard gun runs hot enough to melt right through them.
- Hold pieces still for the first 30–60 seconds; hot glue grabs fast but needs a moment to fully set.
- It's not gap-filling on load-bearing joints — treat it as a craft and hobby adhesive, not a structural one.
- Glue sticks aren't universal — high-temp and low-temp sticks aren't interchangeable across guns.
