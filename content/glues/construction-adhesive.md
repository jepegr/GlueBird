# Construction Adhesive: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

When a project needs to hold real weight — stone, masonry, structural wood — construction adhesive is built for exactly that, applied from a caulk gun in a thick, gap-filling bead.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- It's genuinely load-bearing enough that some construction adhesives are rated to supplement or even partially replace mechanical fasteners in specific applications.
- One popular brand name for this category became so dominant that people now use it as a generic term for the whole product category, the same way 'Kleenex' stands in for tissues.
- Masonry-rated formulas are specifically engineered to handle the temperature swings that cause many other adhesives to crack outdoors over time.

## Best Uses

- Stone, brick, and concrete bonding
- Heavy wood-to-wood structural joints
- Outdoor projects exposed to real weather

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- Apply in a zig-zag bead rather than a thin line — it's designed to fill gaps, not sit flush like wood glue.
- Clamp or brace the joint until it reaches full cure; don't trust surface tack alone on anything heavy.
- Check the label for a masonry-rated formula specifically if the project lives outdoors.
- Let the full 24–48 hour cure pass before loading the joint — heavy materials punish an early load hardest.
