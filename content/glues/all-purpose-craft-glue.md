# All-Purpose Craft Glue: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

Not every project needs industrial strength. Craft glue is deliberately lightweight — easy to control, low odor, and gentle enough for paper and card that a stronger adhesive would just soak through.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- Most craft glues are PVA-based, chemically related to wood glue, but reformulated thinner and faster-tacking for paper and light materials.
- 'Tacky glue' grabs faster than standard white glue because it has a higher solid content, which is why crafters favor it for anything that needs to hold its position without clamping.
- It's one of the few adhesives specifically formulated to be safe for classroom and kids' use in most consumer formulations.

## Best Uses

- Paper, card, and light scrapbooking projects
- Cork-to-cork bonds and other light porous materials
- Any project where low mess matters more than raw strength

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- A glue stick avoids the wrinkling that liquid craft glue can cause on thin paper.
- Let it dry under light pressure — a stack of books works fine for flat paper projects.
- It's not the right choice for anything structural or load-bearing — reach for wood glue or epoxy instead.
- Tacky formulas grab faster but take just as long to fully cure as standard craft glue.
