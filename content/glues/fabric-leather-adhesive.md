# Fabric / Leather Adhesive: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

Fabric and leather move — they fold, stretch, and flex constantly, which is exactly what a rigid adhesive like epoxy or wood glue can't handle. Fabric and leather adhesives are built to bend with the material instead of fighting it.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- Many fabric adhesives are PVA-based like wood glue, but reformulated to stay flexible once cured instead of hardening rigid.
- Leather's rough, unfinished 'flesh side' actually bonds better than the smooth grain side — it gives the adhesive more surface texture to grip.
- Cyanoacrylate (super glue) is a poor choice for leather despite bonding almost everything else instantly — it goes brittle and can crack the surrounding leather at the fold line.

## Best Uses

- Upholstery repairs and seam fixes
- Bonding fabric linings inside leather goods
- Any joint that will fold or flex repeatedly

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- Apply a thin, even coat — too much soaks through thin fabric and stiffens it visibly.
- Press with a roller rather than fingers for an even bond without a ridge.
- Test on an offcut first; some dyes and finishes react differently to different adhesive formulas.
- Let it cure fully before flexing the joint, even if it feels dry to the touch.
