# Polyurethane Glue: What It's Good For, and How to Get the Most Out of It

Polyurethane glue is the one that surprises first-time users — it doesn't just sit in the joint, it foams and expands as it cures, which is either exactly what you need or a mess you didn't expect.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- Like cyanoacrylate, polyurethane glue cures by reacting with moisture — but instead of setting in seconds, the reaction releases carbon dioxide slowly, which is what causes the foaming.
- The expansion is genuinely useful for filling small gaps between rough or mismatched surfaces, but it also means squeeze-out hardens into a foam that needs to be trimmed, not wiped, once cured.
- Once fully cured, polyurethane glue is fully waterproof — unlike standard PVA, it holds up to real outdoor exposure.

## Best Uses

- Outdoor furniture and anything exposed to weather
- Joints with slightly uneven or gapped surfaces
- Projects where full waterproofing matters more than fast setting

## Tips for a Bond That Lasts

- Dampen one surface slightly before applying — it kicks off the moisture-cure reaction and gives a more even bond.
- Clamp it; the foaming action pushes the pieces apart if there's nothing holding them together.
- Don't use too much — a thin layer is enough, since expansion does the rest.
- Let squeeze-out cure fully, then slice it off with a chisel rather than trying to wipe it away wet.
