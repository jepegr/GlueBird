# How to Glue Rubber to Rubber

**Quick answer:** Contact Cement is the adhesive to reach for. A rubber-specific contact cement stays flexible after curing, so the seam moves with the material instead of cracking.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

A rigid adhesive on two flexible rubber surfaces creates a hard spot that concentrates stress right at the seam — that's usually where the failure starts, not in the rubber itself.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Clean both surfaces with alcohol — rubber often carries a light mold-release residue from manufacturing.
- Apply an even, thin coat to both pieces rather than a thick one to a single side.
- Flex-test a scrap piece first if the rubber type is unknown; not all rubber compounds react the same way.
