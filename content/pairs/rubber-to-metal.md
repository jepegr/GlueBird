# How to Glue Rubber to Metal

**Quick answer:** Contact Cement is the adhesive to reach for. Coat both surfaces, let the cement go tacky before joining — rubber-to-metal bonds fail most often from rushing this step.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Rubber has naturally low surface energy, meaning most liquids — including many glues — struggle to wet and grip it at all without the right adhesive chemistry.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Degrease the metal thoroughly; any residue undermines the bond before it starts.
- Press with firm, even pressure across the whole surface once contact is made — there's no repositioning.
- Choose a rubber-specific contact cement over a general-purpose one for a bond that stays flexible.
