# How to Glue Foam to Foam

**Quick answer:** Foam-Safe Adhesive is the adhesive to reach for. Hot glue works in a pinch but can melt a shallow crater into soft foams — test on an offcut first.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Not all foam is the same — polystyrene, EVA, and polyurethane foam all react differently to solvents, which is why a 'foam-safe' label matters more than a strength rating here.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Use a low-temp glue gun if going the hot-glue route — standard guns run too hot for soft foam.
- A foam-safe spray adhesive gives more even coverage than a bead of glue for large flat panels.
- Weight the pieces evenly while curing rather than clamping hard, which can crush soft foam permanently.
