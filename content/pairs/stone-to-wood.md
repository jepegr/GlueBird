# How to Glue Stone to Wood

**Quick answer:** Construction Adhesive is the adhesive to reach for. Let the adhesive's full cure time pass before any weight goes on the joint — stone's weight punishes an early load.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Wood flexes and breathes with humidity; stone does neither — that mismatch is why a rigid, brittle adhesive tends to fail here even when the initial bond looks solid.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Seal bare wood first if the project will see any moisture — unsealed wood swells and stresses the joint.
- Use a gap-filling bead rather than a thin glue line, since stone surfaces are rarely flat.
- Support the stone externally (a ledge, brace, or clamp) until the adhesive reaches full strength.
