# How to Glue Cork to Wood

**Quick answer:** PVA Wood Glue is the adhesive to reach for. Cork is porous enough that ordinary wood glue soaks in and holds fine — no need for anything stronger.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Cork is remarkably porous for how solid it feels, which is why it plays nicely with basic water-based glues that would struggle on denser or sealed surfaces.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Spread glue thinly and evenly across the cork; it's easy to over-apply on such a soft, absorbent surface.
- Weight the piece flat while it cures rather than clamping hard, which can compress the cork.
- For cork flooring specifically, check for a manufacturer-recommended adhesive rated for foot traffic.
