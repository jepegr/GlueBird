# How to Glue Wood to Metal

**Quick answer:** 2-Part Epoxy is the adhesive to reach for. Rough up the metal with fine sandpaper first — epoxy grips a scuffed surface far better than one straight off the shelf.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Wood is porous and metal is not — an adhesive has to grip two completely different surface types at once, which rules out anything that relies purely on soaking in.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Degrease the metal with alcohol before sanding — oil under the scratches defeats the prep work.
- Let the wood acclimate to room temperature and humidity before bonding for the most stable joint.
- Clamp lightly; too much pressure squeezes epoxy out of a porous wood surface.
