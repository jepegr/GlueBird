# How to Glue Fabric to Metal

**Quick answer:** 2-Part Epoxy is the adhesive to reach for. Roughen the metal side lightly and use a small dab — too much epoxy will soak through and stiffen the fabric.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Metal's smooth, nonporous surface actually makes it one of the more demanding partners for fabric — there's nothing for a soft adhesive to key into, which is why a stronger bonding agent is worth the extra step.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Degrease the metal with alcohol before roughening it — skipping this undermines the whole bond.
- Apply epoxy to the metal side only, then press the fabric on rather than saturating the cloth.
- Let it cure undisturbed on a flat surface; fabric shifts easily before the epoxy sets.
