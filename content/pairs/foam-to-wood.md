# How to Glue Foam to Wood

**Quick answer:** Foam-Safe Adhesive is the adhesive to reach for. Skip anything solvent-based — it will eat through polystyrene or EVA foam within seconds.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Foam-to-wood is a common cosplay and prop-making combo, and it's also where the most projects get ruined — not by a weak bond, but by a glue that melts the foam on contact.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Test any new adhesive on a foam offcut before committing to the real piece.
- Clamp lightly rather than heavily — foam compresses easily under pressure.
- If the wood side is painted or sealed, treat it more like a nonporous surface and add light sanding for grip.
