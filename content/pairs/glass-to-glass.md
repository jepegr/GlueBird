# How to Glue Glass to Glass

**Quick answer:** Cyanoacrylate (Super Glue) is the adhesive to reach for. For small pieces, gel-type CA sets almost instantly; switch to epoxy if the joint has any gap to fill.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Glass is one of the few materials where cyanoacrylate genuinely shines — its low viscosity lets it wick into a hairline crack that a thicker adhesive like epoxy would never reach.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Hold pieces in exact alignment for the full set time — CA doesn't forgive repositioning.
- Wipe excess immediately; cured CA on glass is nearly impossible to remove without scratching.
- For decorative or stained glass, use a UV-cure resin instead if crystal-clear results matter.
