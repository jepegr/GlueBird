# How to Glue Stone to Stone

**Quick answer:** Construction Adhesive is the adhesive to reach for. For anything load-bearing, a masonry-rated construction adhesive outperforms epoxy once temperatures swing outdoors.

## Why This Combination Needs the Right Adhesive

Stone and concrete are heavier and more thermally variable outdoors than almost any other common material pairing — a joint that holds fine indoors can fail after one winter outside.

## Step-by-Step Tips

- Clean dust and loose debris off both surfaces thoroughly — stone sheds grit that undermines adhesion.
- Apply in a thick, gap-filling bead rather than a thin line; stone surfaces are rarely perfectly flat.
- Brace or weight the joint until full cure — stone's weight alone can shift an early-set bond.
